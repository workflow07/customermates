## Why Marketing Teams Need a CRM

A CRM for marketing is the system that turns scattered lead data into measurable revenue impact. Marketing does not end when a lead is generated. The handoff from marketing to sales is where most companies lose momentum, and revenue. Without a shared system, marketing generates leads that sales never follows up on, campaign performance cannot be tied to actual closed deals, and contact data lives in disconnected silos across a dozen tools.

This is not just an efficiency problem, it is a revenue problem. According to [HubSpot's State of Marketing report](https://www.hubspot.com/state-of-marketing), aligning marketing and sales teams around shared data is one of the top drivers of revenue growth. A [CRM strategy](/blog/crm-strategy) built on a shared platform is the foundation that makes this alignment possible.

Customermates gives marketing teams the [contact management](/features/contact-management), segmentation capabilities, [pipeline](/features/pipeline) visibility, and [workflow automation](/features/workflow-automation) they need to prove their impact, and continuously improve it.

## Key Challenges for Marketing Teams Without a CRM

Marketing teams face specific challenges that a project management tool or standalone email platform cannot solve:

- **Lead handoff gaps cost revenue**: Marketing generates leads through campaigns, events, content, and ads. Without a CRM, those leads sit in a spreadsheet or email tool and never reach sales in time. By the time someone follows up, the prospect has moved on.

- **No campaign-to-revenue attribution**: You know how many leads a campaign generated, but not how many converted to customers and revenue. Without connecting campaign data to [pipeline](/features/pipeline) and deal data, proving marketing ROI is guesswork.

- **Contact data lives in silos**: Your email marketing tool has one contact list, your event platform another, your [sales team](/for/b2b) a third. None of them are synchronized, leading to duplicate records, outdated information, and missed segmentation opportunities.

- **Segmentation is limited by scattered data**: You want to target contacts with personalized campaigns based on industry, company size, engagement history, or deal stage. But when data is spread across five tools, effective segmentation is nearly impossible.

- **Proving marketing ROI is an uphill battle**: Without linking campaigns to pipeline movement and closed revenue, justifying marketing spend to leadership becomes a constant struggle. See our guide on [measuring CRM ROI](/blog/crm-roi) to understand the metrics that matter.

- **GDPR compliance for marketing contacts**: Marketing contact data, email addresses, company information, behavioral data, falls squarely under GDPR. You need a CRM that handles consent, data retention, and privacy by design. Customermates is [GDPR-native and EU-hosted](/features/cloud-crm).

### How do we track which campaigns generate revenue?

Use custom fields to tag contacts and deals with campaign source, channel, and campaign name. As leads move through your [pipeline](/features/pipeline), the campaign attribution travels with them. When a deal closes, you can trace it back to the original marketing campaign, giving you true campaign-to-revenue attribution that goes beyond vanity metrics.
