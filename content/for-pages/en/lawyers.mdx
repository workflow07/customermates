## Why lawyers need a CRM

As a lawyer, your practice depends on relationships. Whether you handle family law, immigration, criminal defense, or estate planning, your clients come through referrals, word of mouth, and repeat business. Yet most solo lawyers and small-practice attorneys manage these relationships with spreadsheets, sticky notes, and memory, losing track of leads, forgetting follow-ups, and leaving money on the table.

A CRM for lawyers changes that. It gives you a single system to track every client, every case, every referral source, and every billable interaction. Instead of juggling tools, you get one place that keeps your solo practice running smoothly.

Unlike [CRM solutions built for large law firms](/for/law-firms), Customermates is designed to work for individual lawyers and small practices, without the complexity, training overhead, or enterprise pricing that makes most legal CRM software impractical for solo practitioners.

### Pain points a lawyer CRM solves

- **Leads slipping through the cracks**: A potential client calls, you are in court, and the note gets lost. Without a system, that consultation never happens.
- **No referral tracking**: A colleague has sent you five clients this year, but you have no record and no way to reciprocate or thank them properly.
- **Scattered client information**: Case notes in a folder, emails in your inbox, billing in accounting software. No single view of a client relationship.
- **Missed follow-ups**: Former clients who were satisfied could return or refer others, but you never stay in touch after the matter closes.
- **Time tracking gaps**: Billable hours get lost because you reconstruct time entries at the end of the week instead of logging them as they happen.
- **Conflict of interest checks**: Before accepting a new client, you need to search your entire client history quickly and accurately.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is a CRM for lawyers?

A CRM (client relationship management) for lawyers is software that helps attorneys manage client relationships, track cases, handle intake, and organize referrals. Unlike general-purpose CRMs, a lawyer CRM is configured for legal workflows, tracking matters, managing conflict checks, and maintaining the structured records that legal practice demands.

### Do solo lawyers really need a CRM?

Yes. Solo lawyers benefit the most from a CRM because they handle every aspect of their practice alone. Without a system, leads get lost, follow-ups get missed, and referral relationships go untracked. A CRM for lawyers automates the administrative work that would otherwise require a dedicated intake coordinator or marketing assistant.
