## Why insurance companies need a dedicated CRM

A CRM for insurance companies is not the same as a CRM for individual agents. Insurance carriers, MGAs, and mid-size insurers operate at a fundamentally different scale, managing thousands of policyholders, coordinating underwriting teams, routing claims through multi-stage pipelines, and maintaining relationships with an entire network of agencies and brokers. Without a purpose-built insurance company CRM, these operations fragment across disconnected tools, creating compliance blind spots and operational bottlenecks.

According to [McKinsey's research on insurance operations](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/financial-services/our-insights/insurance), insurers that digitize core workflows, underwriting, claims, and distribution, see 20-30% improvement in operational efficiency and measurably higher policyholder retention.

Here are the challenges that insurance CRM software solves at the company level:

- **Policyholder data lives in silos**, Policy administration systems hold coverage details. Email holds communication history. Spreadsheets track renewals. No single system gives a 360-degree view of each policyholder relationship.
- **Claims pipelines lack visibility**, When a claim moves from first notice of loss through investigation, adjustment, and settlement, multiple teams touch it. Without a centralized pipeline, claims stall and policyholders experience delays.
- **Underwriting workflows are manual**, Submissions arrive from brokers and agents, but routing, risk assessment, and approval steps happen across email and shared drives. Turnaround times suffer.
- **Agency and broker relationships are unmanaged**, Your distribution network is a revenue engine, but without systematic tracking of broker performance, appointment status, and communication history, top producers feel neglected.
- **Compliance and audit trails are incomplete**, Regulators require documentation of underwriting decisions, claims handling procedures, and policyholder communications. Scattered data means audit risk.
- **Cross-sell and upsell intelligence is missing**, A commercial client with general liability may need workers' compensation or professional liability. Without data linking policyholders to their full coverage portfolio, revenue opportunities go unnoticed.

### Claims pipeline management

Claims are the moment of truth for any insurance company. Customermates lets you build a dedicated claims pipeline on your [visual Kanban board](/features/pipeline) that tracks every claim from first notice through resolution:

**Example claims pipeline stages:**
- First Notice of Loss (FNOL) received
- Claim acknowledged and assigned
- Investigation in progress
- Adjuster review and assessment
- Settlement offer prepared
- Negotiation / dispute
- Settlement approved and paid
- Claim closed

Each claim is a deal with custom fields for claim number, date of loss, reserve amount, adjuster assigned, claimant details, and settlement amount. Filter by claim type, status, age, or reserve size to identify bottlenecks and prioritize high-value or high-urgency claims.

### Underwriting workflow coordination

Submissions from agents and brokers flow into an underwriting pipeline where your team can evaluate, price, and approve or decline business systematically:

**Example underwriting pipeline stages:**

With [workflow automation](/features/workflow-automation), route submissions automatically based on line of business, premium size, or risk category. Set SLA reminders so no submission sits unanswered beyond your target turnaround time.
