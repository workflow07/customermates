## Why Therapists Need a CRM

A CRM for therapists is not clinical software. It does not replace your EHR, practice management system, or session documentation. It handles the business side — the client inquiries that come in, the referral relationships that keep your caseload full, and the follow-ups that turn interest into booked appointments.

Whether you are a psychotherapist, counselor, marriage and family therapist, or mental health professional in private practice, you face the same operational challenges:

- **Client inquiries slip through the cracks**: Someone fills out your contact form or leaves a voicemail. You intend to call back but get pulled into sessions. Days pass and the inquiry goes cold. [Research shows](https://hbr.org/2011/03/the-short-life-of-online-sales-leads) that responding within 5 minutes makes you 100x more likely to connect with a lead.
- **Referral sources are untracked**: Physicians, other therapists, clinics, and insurance networks send clients your way — but you have no system to track which sources perform or to nurture those relationships.
- **Waitlist management is manual**: Who is waiting, what they need, and when to follow up is tracked on sticky notes, spreadsheets, or in your head.
- **Insurance and billing coordination is scattered**: Client insurance details, copay information, and billing notes are spread across different tools with no central view.
- **Session notes for relationship context are lost**: Not clinical notes — the business context. What a client mentioned about scheduling preferences, who referred them, what communication style they prefer.

A therapist CRM centralizes all of this. It gives you a single place to manage client intake, referrals, and the operational side of your therapy practice — so you spend less time on admin and more time in sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a CRM for therapists?

A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) for therapists is software that manages the business side of a therapy practice — client inquiries, referral tracking, intake pipelines, follow-ups, and communication history. It is not clinical software. It does not store treatment plans, session notes, or health records. It handles the operational tasks that keep your caseload full and your referral network active.

### Is a therapist CRM the same as practice management software?

No. Practice management software typically handles scheduling, clinical documentation, billing, and insurance claims. A CRM for therapists focuses on the relationship and business development side — tracking new inquiries, managing your referral pipeline, automating follow-ups, and organizing client communication. Customermates is designed to complement your existing practice management or EHR system, not replace it.

### Is Customermates HIPAA-compliant?

Customermates is a business CRM, not a clinical system. It is designed to handle non-clinical business data — inquiries, referral sources, scheduling preferences, and communication history. It is GDPR-native and EU-hosted in Germany. For practices that need full data sovereignty, [self-hosting](/features/self-hosted) gives you complete control over where your data lives and how it is secured. Customermates does not store protected health information (PHI) or clinical records.

### Can I connect Customermates to my scheduling tool?

Yes. Customermates has a full API and integrates with [n8n workflow automation](/features/workflow-automation) to connect with tools like Calendly, Acuity Scheduling, Google Calendar, and most practice management systems. When a client books or cancels, the CRM record updates automatically.

### How is Customermates different from using a spreadsheet?

Spreadsheets have no automation, no pipeline visualization, no email integration, and no way to set follow-up reminders. A CRM for therapy practices gives you a visual [pipeline](/features/pipeline) to track every inquiry, automated tasks that ensure no follow-up is missed, and a complete communication history for every contact. Once you have more than 10-15 active inquiries, a spreadsheet becomes a liability.

### Can I use Customermates for a group therapy practice?

Yes. Customermates supports multiple users at €10/user/month. Each therapist can manage their own pipeline while the practice manager has visibility across all caseloads. Shared referral source tracking means the entire practice benefits from relationship data, not just individual therapists.

### What data should I put in a therapist CRM vs. my EHR?

Your EHR handles clinical data — treatment plans, session notes, diagnoses, and health records. Your CRM handles business data — inquiry source, referral relationships, communication preferences, insurance details for coordination, waitlist status, and follow-up tasks. Think of it this way: if it helps you get and retain clients, it belongs in the CRM. If it is part of clinical care, it belongs in the EHR.
