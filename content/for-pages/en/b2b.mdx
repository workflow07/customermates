## What is a B2B CRM and why does your team need one?

A B2B CRM (business-to-business customer relationship management) is software designed to help companies that sell to other companies manage accounts, track deals across longer sales cycles, and coordinate multi-stakeholder relationships. Unlike a B2C CRM that handles high-volume, short-cycle transactions, a B2B CRM system focuses on fewer deals with higher value, deeper account hierarchies, and more complex buying committees.

If your sales team juggles spreadsheets, loses follow-ups in email threads, or cannot tell you which deals will close this quarter, you need a proper CRM for B2B. Customermates is an open-source B2B CRM software that gives you account-based selling tools, pipeline automation, and native AI integration, hosted in the EU, GDPR-native, and ready in two minutes.

## Why B2B sales are different: and why generic CRMs fail

B2B selling is fundamentally different from B2C, and a CRM that ignores those differences creates more friction than it removes. Here is what makes B2B unique:

### Longer sales cycles

Enterprise deals take weeks, months, or even quarters to close. Your CRM needs to track every touchpoint across that timeline, emails, calls, meetings, proposals, without anything falling through the cracks. Customermates gives you a [pipeline view](/features/pipeline) that maps every deal stage from initial outreach to signed contract.

### Multiple stakeholders per deal

In B2B, you rarely sell to one person. A typical deal involves procurement, technical evaluators, department heads, and C-level sponsors. Customermates lets you link multiple contacts to a single deal and organization, so you always know who is involved, what their role is, and where they stand. Use the [contact management](/features/contact-management) module to track every stakeholder relationship.

### Account-based selling and company hierarchies

B2B CRM software must support account-based selling, focusing sales and marketing efforts on target accounts rather than individual leads. Customermates models company hierarchies with the [account management](/features/account-management) feature, letting you map parent companies, subsidiaries, and divisions. Assign contacts to the right level, track account-level revenue, and coordinate outreach across the entire buying organization.

### Higher deal values and complex negotiations

When deals are worth five or six figures, every detail matters. Custom fields let you track contract terms, procurement requirements, competitor activity, and technical specifications alongside the standard deal data.

### Integration with business tools

B2B companies rely on ERP systems, accounting software, marketing automation, and project management tools. A disconnected CRM becomes a data silo. Customermates connects to your existing stack through a full REST API and webhooks, and the native [n8n workflow automation](https://n8n.io/) integration lets you build any integration without writing code.

## B2B CRM vs. B2C CRM: key differences

Understanding the distinction helps you choose the right tool:

| Aspect | B2B CRM | B2C CRM |
|---|---|---|
| **Sales cycle length** | Weeks to months | Minutes to days |
| **Deal value** | Higher (often 4-6 figures) | Lower (individual transactions) |
| **Decision-makers** | Multiple stakeholders, buying committees | Usually one person |
| **Account structure** | Company hierarchies, subsidiaries | Individual customers |
| **Relationship focus** | Long-term, account-based | Transactional, volume-based |
| **Key features** | Account management, deal stages, multi-contact linking | Segmentation, campaigns, loyalty programs |

Customermates is built for the left column. If your sales process involves account-based selling, multi-stakeholder negotiations, and deal cycles measured in weeks rather than minutes, this is the B2B CRM system designed for you.

## Deal stages for B2B sales: a practical framework

Most B2B companies benefit from pipeline stages that reflect the actual buying journey:

1. **Lead qualified**, The prospect matches your ideal customer profile and has expressed interest.
2. **Discovery**, You have scheduled a first meeting to understand the prospect's needs, budget, and timeline.
3. **Proposal sent**, A formal proposal or quote has been delivered.
4. **Negotiation**, Terms, pricing, or scope are being discussed with procurement or leadership.
5. **Legal / contract review**, The deal is in the hands of legal teams on both sides.
6. **Closed-won**, Contract signed, deal closed, onboarding begins.
7. **Closed-lost**, The deal did not close. Record the reason to improve future efforts.

Customermates lets you customize these stages entirely. Add, rename, or reorder them to match your specific B2B sales process.
