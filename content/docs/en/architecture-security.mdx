## A simple overview

Customermates is built so non-technical teams can use it confidently while technical teams still get a robust foundation.  
You can think of the platform as two clear layers: the application experience and the secure data core. External native AI integration, automation platforms, and custom integrations reach the data core only through authenticated, user-scoped APIs. This is the right page when you need a high-level view of CRM architecture, data isolation, and how external access is scoped.

## How your data is handled

Your CRM data is managed in the core platform with strict access boundaries. External integrations (API, MCP, n8n) authenticate with user-scoped API keys, so any automated client runs with the user's own permissions rather than broad shared access.

## Why this matters for your team

You get a setup that feels simple from the outside, but is designed for trust and control underneath. Business teams can focus on workflows and customer work, while technical stakeholders can rely on clear tenant isolation and audit trails. For the integration surfaces built on top of that foundation, continue with [CRM Integrations](/docs/integrations-intro), [Model Context Protocol (MCP)](/docs/mcp), [Permissions & Roles](/docs/features-permissions-roles), or [Webhooks & Events](/docs/features-webhooks-events).
