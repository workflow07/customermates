If you want an AI-first CRM that you can self-host, is open source, and doesn't bolt automation on as an afterthought, Customermates is the closest match. Bigger ecosystems win on marketplace breadth; we win on agent-native design and price.

## Where each one wins

**Pipedrive** wins on sales-pipeline polish and the Chrome extension. If your team's entire workflow is drag-deals-between-stages and you don't care about AI, Pipedrive is still very good. It doesn't offer self-hosting or MCP.

**HubSpot** wins on ecosystem breadth. If you need marketing, service desk, and CMS under one roof and budget is not a concern, HubSpot is hard to beat on breadth. Pricing ramps fast once you grow.

**Airtable** wins on schema flexibility. It's not really a CRM, and you'll rebuild things CRMs give you for free, but the flexibility is unmatched.

**EspoCRM and Twenty** win on cost if you self-host and don't need MCP. Both are capable open-source CRMs. Neither is designed for AI-driven workflows.

**Customermates** wins when you want:

- Your AI (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor) to update the CRM in conversation.
- To self-host on EU infra without losing features.
- A small, opinionated surface instead of a maze of settings.
- Open source under AGPLv3, with a separate commercial license for the Enterprise components.

## What we're honest about

- We have **fewer marketplace integrations** than HubSpot or Pipedrive. Webhooks, n8n, and MCP cover most of what teams actually build.
- We don't do **marketing automation, email campaigns, or landing pages**. We are a CRM, not a suite.
- We don't have **built-in phone integrations** today.
- **Mobile app** is web-responsive, not native.

If any of those are dealbreakers, be honest with yourself about the tradeoff.
