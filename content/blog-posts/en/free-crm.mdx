# Free CRM Software: The Best Free CRM Systems Compared

Free CRM software is the fastest way to move from spreadsheets to structured customer management. For solo founders, freelancers, and small teams, a free CRM can be the difference between losing track of leads and closing deals consistently. But free CRM tools have limits, and the hidden costs often outweigh what you save.

This article compares the best free CRM systems, explains where each one falls short, and shows why a free CRM freeware model is not always the cheapest choice.

## The 7 Best Free CRM Systems

### 1. HubSpot CRM Free

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: HubSpot CRM (hubspot.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/hubspot-crm.png" />

[HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com/products/crm) offers one of the most feature-rich free CRM versions available. The free plan is unlimited in time and supports up to two users.

**What you get for free:**

- Up to 1,000,000 contacts
- Basic pipeline management
- Email tracking and templates
- Meeting scheduler
- Basic reporting and AI features

**Limitations:** No automation workflows, HubSpot branding on forms and emails, no phone support. Data stored in the US, a [GDPR](https://gdpr.eu/) concern for European businesses. Upgrade starts at $90/user/month.

### 2. Zoho CRM Free Edition

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: Zoho CRM Free (zoho.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/zoho-crm.png" />

[Zoho CRM](https://www.zoho.com/crm/free-crm.html) offers a free version for up to three users with contact and lead management, tasks, and basic reporting.

**Strengths:** Broad feature set within the Zoho ecosystem, mobile app.

**Limitations:** Only 3 users, no workflow rules, limited automation, no AI access. Upgrade from $14/user/month.

### 3. Bitrix24

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: Bitrix24 (bitrix24.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/bitrix24.png" />

[Bitrix24](https://www.bitrix24.com) combines CRM with project management and internal communication tools. The free version allows unlimited users.

**Strengths:** Unlimited users, integrated project management, chat and video calls.

**Limitations:** 5 GB storage, cluttered interface, limited automation, performance issues with large datasets. Upgrade from $49/month (team license).

### 4. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue)

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: Brevo (brevo.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/brevo.png" />

[Brevo](https://www.brevo.com) combines CRM with email marketing and sales automation. The free plan includes 300 emails per day.

**Strengths:** Strong email marketing features, deal pipeline, unlimited contacts.

**Limitations:** Daily email limit, Brevo branding on emails, limited reporting. More of a marketing tool than a full CRM.

### 5. Freshsales Free

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: Freshsales (freshworks.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/freshsales.png" />

[Freshsales](https://www.freshworks.com/crm/sales/) offers a free tier with basic contact and deal management plus a mobile app.

**Strengths:** Clean interface, part of the Freshworks ecosystem.

**Limitations:** No sales sequences, very limited automation, few reports. Upgrade from $9/user/month.

### 6. CentralStationCRM

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: CentralStationCRM (centralstationcrm.de, April 2026)" src="competitors/centralstation-crm.png" />

[CentralStationCRM](https://centralstationcrm.de) is a German-made CRM popular with small teams. Free for up to 3 users and 200 contacts.

**Strengths:** Simple UI, German-language support, data hosted in Germany.

**Limitations:** Maximum 200 contacts and 20 MB storage. Too small for growing teams.

### 7. SuiteCRM (Open Source)

<MarkdownImage alt="Screenshot: SuiteCRM (suitecrm.com, April 2026)" src="competitors/suitecrm.png" />

[SuiteCRM](https://suitecrm.com) is a powerful open-source Salesforce alternative. The entire source code is freely available.

**Strengths:** Full feature set, no user limits, complete data control.

**Limitations:** Requires self-hosting and technical expertise for setup and maintenance. No official commercial support. The UI feels dated.

## Three Types of Free CRM Software

Not every free CRM works the same way. There are three models:

### Freemium CRM

Most free CRM systems use the freemium model. You get a limited version for free, once your team grows or you need automation, you must upgrade to a paid plan. HubSpot, Zoho, and Freshsales all use this model.

The catch: the free version is intentionally limited to push you toward an upgrade. And when you do, costs often jump dramatically.

### Open-Source CRM

Open-source CRM systems like [SuiteCRM](https://suitecrm.com), [Twenty](https://twenty.com), and Customermates publish their entire source code. You can download, self-host, and customize the software freely. No artificial limits, no vendor lock-in.

All three can be deployed via [Docker](https://www.docker.com/) on your own infrastructure, for example on [Hetzner](https://www.hetzner.com/) or any cloud provider.

### Ad-Supported or Data-Driven CRM

Some providers fund their free CRM through advertising or monetizing your customer data. Be cautious here, if your CRM provider profits from your customer data, you may have a GDPR compliance problem.

## The Hidden Costs of Free CRM Software

Using a CRM system for free sounds great. But "free" does not mean "without cost":

### Time Lost to Missing Automation

Free CRM versions rarely include automation. If your team spends 30 minutes daily on manual tasks that a proper CRM handles automatically, that's over 10 hours per month. At $50/hour, that's $500/month, far more than any affordable [CRM system](/blog/crm-system) costs.

### Forced Upgrades

Most freemium CRMs limit user count, storage, or features. When your team grows from three to five people, costs often triple overnight. HubSpot jumps from free to $90/user/month, that's $450/month for a 5-person team.

### GDPR Risks with US Hosting

Many free CRM providers store data in the US. For European businesses processing personal customer data, this is a serious [GDPR risk](https://gdpr.eu/fines/). Fines for violations can reach €20 million or 4% of annual revenue.

### Migration Costs

The longer you use a free CRM, the more expensive switching becomes later. Data export, field mapping, workflow rebuilding, and team retraining can take weeks and cost thousands.

## When Does a Free CRM Make Sense?

A free CRM is the right choice in three situations:

- **Solo operators with few contacts:** Under 100 contacts, no team, simple [contact management](/features/contact-management) is enough.
- **Trial before buying:** Use the free version to evaluate a CRM's usability before committing.
- **Non-commercial projects:** Clubs or side projects where GDPR and scalability are not critical.

Once you have more than two people in sales, need [workflow automation](/features/workflow-automation), or must comply with GDPR, free CRM systems hit their limits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there a truly free CRM?

HubSpot, Zoho, and Freshsales offer permanently free versions with limited features. Open-source alternatives like SuiteCRM are completely free but require self-hosting. Customermates offers the full feature set for €10/user/month as a managed service, or free to self-host.

### Can I use a free CRM for my team?

Yes, but with restrictions. Most free CRM systems limit users to two or three. For teams of four or more, a paid plan is almost always necessary.

### Is free CRM software GDPR compliant?

Not automatically. What matters is where your customer data is stored. CRM providers with US-only hosting meet GDPR requirements only partially. Look for EU hosting or a self-hosting option.

### Which free CRM is best for startups?

For the very first weeks, a free CRM can work. Once you're actively acquiring customers and your sales process grows, you need automation and unlimited capacity. A [CRM for startups](/for/startups) with fair pricing is cheaper than upgrading from a freemium provider.
