## Best-of-Breed CRM vs. All-in-One Suite

One of the most important strategic decisions is whether to buy a specialized CRM or a platform that bundles CRM with other business tools.

**Best-of-breed CRM** (Pipedrive, Close, Customermates): Does one thing well. Connects to other tools via integrations or automation.

- Pro: Better at its core job, lower cost, easier to switch
- Con: Requires integration work, multiple vendor relationships

**All-in-one suite** (HubSpot, Zoho, NetSuite, Dynamics 365): Bundles CRM with marketing, service, ERP, or other modules.

- Pro: Single vendor, unified data, less integration needed
- Con: Higher cost, vendor lock-in, features may be shallower than specialists

**The middle ground**: Customermates with n8n automation bridges this gap. It is a focused CRM with a built-in visual automation engine that connects to hundreds of external tools without third-party middleware costs.

## How to Build Your Own CRM Comparison Spreadsheet

If you want to evaluate CRMs against your own criteria, here is a practical framework you can replicate in any spreadsheet tool.

### Columns to Include

1. **CRM name**
2. **Monthly cost per user** (at the tier you actually need)
3. **Annual cost for your team size**
4. **3-year total** (include implementation estimates)
5. **Must-have features** (score 1 = missing, 2 = partial, 3 = full)
6. **Nice-to-have features** (same scoring)
7. **Data privacy score** (open source, EU hosting, GDPR, self-hosting)
8. **Ease of use** (rate after trial)
9. **Weighted total score**

### Process

1. List your 5-7 must-have features as rows
2. Weight each feature by importance (e.g., pipeline management = 10, audit logging = 3)
3. Trial your top 3 candidates for at least one week each with real data
4. Have each team member score independently, then average
5. Multiply scores by weights and compare totals

This structured approach removes emotion and vendor influence from the decision.
