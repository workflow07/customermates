## Pipedrive vs HubSpot: quick overview

Pipedrive launched in 2010 as a CRM built by salespeople for salespeople. Its drag-and-drop pipeline boards remain its core strength. The platform is intentionally narrow — focused on deals and activities rather than marketing or service.

HubSpot started as an inbound marketing platform in 2006 and expanded into CRM, sales, service, and operations. Its free CRM tier is the industry's most effective lead magnet, but the path from free to functional is one of the steepest pricing cliffs in SaaS. HubSpot also charges a mandatory onboarding fee of $1,500 or more for Professional plans.

The core trade-off: Pipedrive offers simplicity at moderate cost. HubSpot offers breadth at premium cost. Customermates offers both simplicity and depth at the lowest cost.

## What is HubSpot's biggest competitor?

HubSpot's biggest competitor depends on the segment. In the mid-market CRM space, Pipedrive and Zoho CRM are the most direct competitors. For marketing automation, Marketo and ActiveCampaign compete head-to-head. For the all-in-one platform play, Salesforce is the primary rival. Customermates competes as the modern, affordable alternative for teams that need CRM without the complexity or cost escalation of HubSpot's tiered model.

## Feature comparison

### Pipeline and deal management

Pipedrive's visual pipeline is its signature feature. Drag-and-drop deal boards, activity-based selling, and deal rotting indicators make it intuitive for sales teams. However, the number of open deals is capped by plan (3,000 on Essential, up to unlimited on Enterprise at $99/mo).

HubSpot provides deal management with a clean interface, but pipeline views feel secondary to the broader platform. The free and Starter plans limit you to 2 deal pipelines. Pipeline customization improves at Professional and above.

Customermates combines Pipedrive-style visual pipelines with unlimited deals, contacts, organizations, and pipelines at the base price. No caps at any level.

### Marketing and inbound tools

This is HubSpot's clear advantage. Email campaigns, landing pages, A/B testing, blog management, SEO tools, and marketing automation are deeply integrated. For marketing-driven teams, HubSpot is genuinely powerful — at Professional pricing ($100/mo) and above.

Pipedrive is not a marketing platform. It offers basic email campaigns (via add-on) and a web forms feature, but teams that need marketing tools typically pair Pipedrive with a dedicated marketing solution.

Customermates focuses on CRM, not marketing automation. For teams that need marketing tools, it integrates with dedicated platforms via n8n — keeping CRM clean and marketing flexible.

### Automation and workflows

This is where both platforms show their tier-gating most clearly.

**Pipedrive:** Workflow automation starts at the Advanced plan ($34/mo) with 30 active automations. Professional allows 60. Power allows 100. Only Enterprise ($99/mo) removes the cap. The automation builder is straightforward but limited in complexity.

**HubSpot:** The free and Starter plans offer almost no workflow capability. Meaningful automation — sequences, custom workflows, lead rotation — requires Professional at $100/mo. The automation builder is excellent once you reach that tier.

**Customermates:** Unlimited workflow automation via n8n from day one. No caps, no tier requirements. Connect to thousands of services without restrictions.

### AI capabilities

Pipedrive's AI Sales Assistant provides deal insights and activity suggestions. It is available as a feature on higher plans but remains relatively basic.

HubSpot's AI tools are distributed across the platform — content assistants, predictive lead scoring, and forecasting. The most valuable AI features require Professional or Enterprise plans.

Customermates includes AI agents at the base EUR 10 price. No tier upgrade required.

### Reporting and analytics

Pipedrive reserves custom reports and revenue forecasting for Professional ($49/mo). Lower plans provide standard dashboards only.

HubSpot locks custom reporting behind Professional ($100/mo). Free and Starter plans offer only canned reports. The reporting engine at Professional is strong but expensive to reach.

Customermates provides full reporting capabilities at the flat EUR 10 price.

### Customer support

Pipedrive offers email and chat support on all plans, with phone support starting at Power ($64/mo). Response times are generally quick for a mid-market CRM.

HubSpot provides email and in-app chat support on paid plans, with phone support from Professional ($100/mo). HubSpot Academy is a strong self-service resource with free courses and certifications.

Customermates provides direct support and full documentation. As an open-source platform, you also benefit from community support and full code transparency.

### Mobile experience

Both Pipedrive and HubSpot offer mobile apps for iOS and Android. Pipedrive's mobile app excels at quick deal updates and activity logging on the go. HubSpot's app mirrors the desktop breadth, which is comprehensive but can feel heavy for quick CRM tasks. Customermates provides a responsive mobile experience with full CRM access at the same flat price.

HubSpot processes data primarily through US-based infrastructure. EU data residency is offered for some data types on certain plans, but it is not comprehensive. GDPR compliance relies on data processing agreements rather than guaranteed data localization.

Customermates hosts exclusively in Germany and is GDPR/DSGVO native by design. Self-hosting is available for full data sovereignty. No cross-border routing, no partial data residency, no compliance uncertainty.

Customermates connects to thousands of services through built-in n8n automation, with a fully open API. No marketplace lock-in, no ecosystem dependency.
