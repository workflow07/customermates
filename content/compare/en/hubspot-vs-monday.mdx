## What CRM is better than HubSpot?

The answer depends on what "better" means for your team. HubSpot excels at marketing-to-sales alignment with native email campaigns, A/B testing, lead capture forms, and AI-assisted dynamic scoring. No other CRM matches its integrated marketing depth.

But HubSpot's strengths come at a steep price. The jump from Free to Starter ($15/seat/month) is modest. The jump from Starter to Professional ($1,170/month for 5 seats) is where most teams feel the squeeze. Custom reporting, advanced automation, sequences, and A/B testing all require Professional. Enterprise starts at $4,300/month for 7 seats. Onboarding fees of $3,000 to $8,000 add to the initial investment.

For teams that need CRM without the marketing platform overhead, several alternatives outperform HubSpot on cost and simplicity:
- **Customermates** delivers complete CRM at €10/user/month — contacts, deals, pipelines, AI agents, automation, email integration — with EU hosting and open-source transparency
- **Pipedrive** provides strong pipeline management for sales-focused teams
- **Zoho CRM** offers broad functionality at a mid-range price

The key question is whether you truly need HubSpot's marketing tools integrated with your CRM, or whether you are paying for platform complexity you do not use.

## The HubSpot cost problem

HubSpot's free CRM is one of the best entry points in the market. Contact management, basic deal tracking, and email logging work well at zero cost for up to 2 users. The problem starts when your team needs more.

The Starter plan at $15/seat/month is reasonable. But Professional at $1,170/month (5 seats included) is where sticker shock hits. Features most teams consider standard — custom reporting, advanced workflow automation, email sequences, sales forecasting — all require Professional. Enterprise at $4,300/month (7 seats) adds predictive lead scoring, advanced permissions, and custom objects.

For a 10-person sales team on Professional with Marketing Hub add-ons, monthly costs routinely exceed $2,000. HubSpot's pricing model rewards starting on the platform but penalizes growth through tier escalation, per-contact charges for marketing features, and mandatory onboarding fees.

## The Monday CRM depth problem

Monday CRM is approachable and visually satisfying. Teams set up pipelines quickly using the familiar board interface. For lightweight deal tracking alongside project management, it works. Monday CRM includes 16 preset CRM automations and customizable lead scoring on lower tiers.

The limitation surfaces when teams need real CRM depth. Monday CRM pricing starts with a 3-seat minimum: Basic at $36/month, Standard at $51/month, Pro at $84/month. Individual seat prices are $12, $17, and $28 respectively. Standard is required for email integration and automation (capped at 250 actions/month). Pro unlocks advanced reporting and 25,000 automation actions per month.

More fundamentally, Monday CRM is built on a project management data model. Contact relationships, organizational hierarchies, service management, and deal scoring are not native concepts. Monday AI is still in beta, generating email content and suggesting tasks but not yet matching the sophistication of HubSpot's AI-assisted scoring. Teams that need these capabilities hit a ceiling that no plan upgrade resolves.

## Migration considerations

**Migrating from HubSpot**: Contact and deal data exports from HubSpot are straightforward for basic objects. Custom properties, workflow automations, and marketing assets require manual recreation. The biggest gain from switching is cost predictability — Customermates eliminates tier anxiety and onboarding fees. The biggest adjustment is leaving HubSpot's marketing tools behind. If your team relies heavily on HubSpot Marketing Hub for email campaigns, A/B testing, and landing pages, consider whether you can replace those functions with dedicated email marketing tools alongside Customermates.

**Migrating from Monday CRM**: Data migration is simpler since Monday CRM stores less complex relationship data. Board structures translate to pipeline configurations. The gain is deeper CRM functionality — organizational hierarchies, native contact management, service tracking, and unlimited automation. The adjustment is separating CRM from project management if your team currently uses both in one workspace.

## Data protection comparison

HubSpot uses a global data infrastructure with primary hosting in the US. EU data processing addendums are available but do not guarantee exclusive EU residency for all data types. The platform's extensive subprocessor list includes US-based companies, creating compliance complexity under GDPR. For European businesses in regulated industries, this creates ongoing audit requirements.

Monday.com hosts data primarily in the US via AWS. Enterprise customers can request EU data residency, but Standard and Pro plans use US infrastructure. The platform's privacy documentation acknowledges international data transfers, leaving a compliance gap for teams on non-Enterprise plans.

Customermates hosts exclusively in Germany. All data processing occurs within the EU. Self-hosting eliminates third-party data processing entirely. For organizations subject to strict GDPR requirements or sector-specific regulations, this removes ambiguity completely.
